## Problem Statement:
  #### Description of healthcare problem being addressed
  Our protoype aims to assit in the diagnoses of sleep deprivation, a human health condition. While driving, sleep deprived individuals experience a symptom of sleep deprivation called fatigue which induces microsleep. 
  Microsleep is a brief period of sleep lasting around 30 seconds cuasing around 600 recent deaths [1] and 50,000 injuries [2]. 
  Our protoype aims to detect a sleep deprived indiviuals increased blinking rate(an inidcator of sleep deprivation [3]) moments before micro sleep occurs. 
  
  #### Target user population
  Our low cost prototype using commercially available off the shelf products is inteded to be used for all individuals around the globe, in an accessible low cost manner.
  
  #### Importance and relevance of the problem
  This is a relevent issue casuing the death of roughly 2 ($600 / 365$)individuals per year
  
## Background & research:  
  #### Existing technologies or solutions
  In recent years car manufacturers have implemented Driver Monitoring Systems(DMS) that track realtime driving driving characteristics to determine if a driver is fatigued. These systems often display audible and visual alerts to alert the driver of their fatigued state. 

  #### Limitations of current approaches
  Current anti sleep devices for drivers include products that are low cost but lack the potential to make microsleep statuses accessible over a network.
  
  #### Justifications for the team’s proposed solution
  Our team's prototype fouses on the potential to accessibly and modularly append other electrical devices that can securly and reliably transmit microsleep states to end devices over a network. We purposefully used commercially off the shelf devices that would act as modular system components in our prototype so that the device could be manufactured in a low cost mannner, regardless of if any one component were to become obsolete.
